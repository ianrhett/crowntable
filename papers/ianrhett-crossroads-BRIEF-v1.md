# The Crossroads Brief v1.0

Ground document for DEBATE 001. This is an OPEN QUESTION, not a position. Neither seat's prior output frames this debate; both prior documents are demoted to input evidence.

## The question

What positioning and business development strategy maximizes Ian's income potential while getting him working closely with people again, doing work that matters?

The client-facing question his practice must answer is literally "How can I help?", answered as right livelihood: Ian paid handsomely to apply his experience to the benefit of humanity. An answer that maximizes income but severs purpose fails. An answer that honors purpose but produces hobby income fails.

## The situation

Roughly 30 years of professional arc: live music on the early web, webguy@apple.com and streaming at Apple, founding and leading CivicActions as a fully remote company before remote was a category, three decades of group facilitation, tech leadership, and event production. Capable of operating in almost any professional-services managerial role.

Then 12 years out of that game, spent on purpose: The Six Week Challenge (wellness program he leads personally), a civic tech portfolio, conservation leadership for Nashville's Highland Rim Forest (Metro Council and Tennessee State Legislature resolutions), community organizing, land stewardship. Nashville-based.

## The tension every answer must resolve

The 12 years must read as a moat, not a gap, without the copy sounding like a 60-year-old fluffing the blanket. Self-mythologizing ("I left at the top of my game to save the world") is the named failure mode. The absence-anxiety has partially dissipated in prior conversations, but no copy has yet solved it for a skeptical buyer.

## Constraints and assets

- Directional operator preference: coach-leaning over fractional COO. Challengeable, with cause.
- The Balance Score: a one-question team ritual (1 to 10), innovated at CivicActions under Ian's leadership, released Creative Commons. A talk exists in 50 and 90 minute versions ("The Missing Metric for Modern Teams"). Ian owns thebalancescore.com. Book ambition via collected stories.
- Circle of Chairs: an earlier direction from prior strategy work, available for reuse or rejection.
- ianrhett.com exists (simple design, staying simple).
- Speed matters: this project was elevated as the portfolio's fastest path to real revenue.

## What a winning answer contains, all six

1. **The positioning.** Who Ian is to the buyer, in buyer language, one sentence.
2. **The buyer.** Who pays handsomely for this and why they pay.
3. **The first offer.** What is sold first and its shape (pricing philosophy, not final numbers).
4. **The BD motion.** How the first five clients actually arrive: network, talk, content, referral, or something else. Concrete, sequenced, startable this month.
5. **The absence treatment.** The actual copy strategy that makes 12 purpose-years a moat a skeptical 40-year-old founder would not smirk at.
6. **The Balance Score's role.** Spearhead, proof artifact, or footnote. Argue it, do not assume it.
