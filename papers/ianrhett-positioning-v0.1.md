# ianrhett.com Position Paper v0.1

Seat: [fable]. Status: PROPOSED. Round 1 of adversarial review. Sol attacks, operator arbitrates. Nothing here enters a workplan until the operator approves specific items.

Input evidence: Sol's unsolicited strategic review addendum (2026-07-12, delivered via operator), operator strategy intake of 2026-07-12 (coach-leaning positioning, Balance Score as signature asset).

## 1. The core claim

ianrhett.com should position Ian as a coach with an instrument, not a friction remover. The instrument is the Balance Score. Everything on the site either sells the coaching relationship or proves the instrument works.

Sol's review is strong on posture and page mechanics and silent on the single most commercially distinct asset Ian has. Sol's unifying idea, releasing trapped possibility, is emotionally true and commercially empty: it is not a noun a buyer can ask for, it cannot be booked, searched, or retold at a dinner party, and every transformation consultant on earth claims some version of it. The Balance Score is the opposite: named, concrete, ritualized, demonstrable in ninety seconds, with a talk already built and a domain already owned.

## 2. Positioning statement

Coach first. Operator credentials as proof, not identity.

Working headline: **The missing metric for modern teams.**

Working subhead: I'm Ian Rhett. I coach leaders and teams doing consequential work. For a decade, every meeting at my company opened with one question, answered 1 to 10, that told the team the truth about itself before the agenda started. It's called the Balance Score. I'll show you what it shows you.

The question's wording is under refinement: from "How well are you managing all of your priorities right now?" toward the truer "How are all of your plates spinning right now?" The paper takes no position on final wording; that is an operator call informed by live-audience testing.

Provenance posture, borrowing Sol's own three-posture logic: the Balance Score was innovated at CivicActions under Ian's leadership and released under Creative Commons. Ian does not claim to own it. He claims to have led the company that proved it, and to be the person who can install it. Ownership is weak posture; stewardship of a gift is commercial posture.

## 3. Offer architecture

Four rungs, coaching-weighted, the COO-shaped work demoted to the bottom rung, not deleted.

1. **The Talk.** "The Missing Metric for Modern Teams," 50 or 90 minutes (delta: Q&A plus priorities-mapping exercise). This is the lead engine and the book engine: every delivery collects Balance-Score-gone-wrong stories. Early deliveries may be unpaid on purpose; stories are the compensation.
2. **Leader coaching.** One to one, for the visionary who needs a thinking partner who will tell the truth.
3. **Team engagement.** Install the Balance Score ritual, then do the alignment and ownership work the scores surface. This is Sol's "team alignment intensive" with a spine.
4. **Embedded partnership.** For teams that want Ian in the room every week. This is where the operator resume earns its keep. Listed last so the site reads coach, not fractional COO.

thebalancescore.com: eventually a one-page canonical home for the CC artifact plus a talk-booking link, with ianrhett.com selling the human. Default recommendation: a section on ianrhett.com now, microsite later, after this positioning survives review.

## 6. Rejected from Sol, with reasons

1. **"Releases trapped possibility" as brand spine.** Unownable, unfalsifiable, unsearchable. Demoted to at most one line of connective tissue explaining why coaching and operational work live in one practice.
2. **Sol's homepage headline** ("The vision is real. The organization needs to catch up."). It sells organizational friction removal, which is the fractional COO frame the operator has already directed away from. It also centers the org; coaching buyers hire for themselves and their people.
3. **Method as abstract three-level reflection** (Sol's hierarchy slot 2). A method without a name is a paragraph. A named method is an asset. The Balance Score takes the slot.

## 7. Expected counterattacks, pre-registered

1. The Balance Score is one Creative Commons question; too thin to carry a practice. Anticipated response: NPS is one question and carries an industry. The asset is the ritual, the stories, and the practitioner, never the IP.
2. "The missing metric" headline could read as an HR-tech product, not a person. Must be tested paired with the Direction A portrait, which is the counterweight.
3. Coach positioning enters a saturated market; the COO frame differentiates. Anticipated response: the instrument is the differentiation. A coach with an instrument beats a coach with a vibe, and beats a COO with a resume.
4. The talk funnel presumes a speaking pipeline that does not exist yet. Conceded in part; the mitigation is that early talks are story-collection for the book, so a slow pipeline still compounds.
