# The Crossroads Brief v2.0

Supersedes v1. Ground document for DEBATE 001.

## Why this document exists

Two frontier models, fresh instances, clear minds, full access to the totality of human written experience, are asked to digest one person's situation and surface the sustainable livelihood, and the obvious next step toward it, that he cannot see for himself. This is not a copywriting exercise and not a homepage review. Prior reviews in this repo answered narrower questions; reading them is optional and inheriting them is discouraged.

## The question

How can Ian sustain himself, paid well, working closely with people again, doing work that matters? The question his practice answers for clients is literally "How can I help?" The question you answer for Ian is: what is the right livelihood he is not seeing on his own?

## The person (evidence, not framing)

Roughly 30 years of arc:

- 1996: got a T1 line installed in 36 hours in a UCLA parking lot for the Apple-sponsored Mission: Impossible premiere party.
- Innovated Apple's first music streaming solution, live music at that, as webguy@apple.com; slept at 5 Infinite Loop for about five months, and that absorption WAS his balance at the time.
- Live music on the early web before that was a thing.
- Founded and led CivicActions as a fully remote company before remote was a category.
- Innovated a video messaging protocol for a handwritten-letter business.
- ChildFund International video messaging prototype: adapted See3 (now GatherVoices) core technology into the established sponsor-sponsoree workflow of a multinational child relief organization; an 18-month pilot.
- The 24-hour designathon.
- 30 years of group facilitation, tech leadership, and event production.

Then 12 years outside professional services, on purpose: The Six Week Challenge (a six-practice wellness program he personally leads), a civic tech portfolio, conservation leadership for Nashville's Highland Rim Forest (recognized by Metro Council and Tennessee State Legislature resolutions), community organizing, land stewardship. Nashville-based.

Self-description that rang true in prior conversations: a follower of the future. He sees things 2 to 20 years ahead. He loves brainstorming and innovating. "That's not how it works" and "that can't be done" are catnip to him. He has a history of innovation in the tech space and does not know how to market that pattern, or whether he should or could.

## Known anxieties (name them, then beat them)

- The 12-year absence: he wants it to read as a moat and fears copy that sounds like a 60-year-old fluffing the blanket.
- The innovation history: proud of it, unsure whether it is marketable at all.

## What the operator wants from each seat

Not a menu. A position: the livelihood and the obvious next step, argued. It must ultimately be actionable: name who pays, for what, why they pay him specifically, and how the first engagements arrive. Challenge any assumption in this brief, including the current coach-over-fractional-COO lean, and including whether "positioning" is even the right frame for the problem. Surprise is a success metric: if the operator could have written your answer himself, the answer failed.
